## What it is

Hormone optimization here means **bioidentical hormone replacement therapy**
— BHRT — delivered through the Biote method, which uses small pellets placed
under the skin rather than a cream, a patch, or a pill.

Amy is a Biote-certified provider. Care starts with a hormone lab draw, so
every decision rests on your actual levels rather than an assumption about
them.

## How the pellets work

A pellet is placed under the skin during a short in-office visit. From there
it releases hormone continuously, then dissolves and is absorbed — there is
nothing to take out later.

What's in a pellet is patient-specific. Biote's pellets contain testosterone
or estrogen, and which applies to you comes out of your lab results, not out
of a category you've been sorted into.

## Who it's generally for

People usually start asking about hormones when something has been
persistently off — fatigue that sleep doesn't fix, poor sleep itself, brain
fog, mood swings, hot flashes, or night sweats.

Noticing a pattern is a reason for a conversation, not a conclusion. Plenty of
things produce those experiences, and whether hormones are part of yours is
what the lab draw and the consultation are for. Amy will say so plainly if the
answer is no.

## For men

Men ask about the same things in different words — low energy through the
afternoon, sleep that isn't restoring anything, a shift in libido.

The route is identical: a lab draw first, then a conversation about what the
numbers show. Where pellets are the right call, the men's pellet is
testosterone, placed the same way and priced above. Where they aren't, Amy
will tell you that instead.

## It starts with labs

Three steps, in this order, every time.

1. **A consultation** with Amy — your history, what you've noticed, and
   whether this line is worth pursuing at all. Free, as always.
2. **A hormone lab draw** — blood work measuring where your levels sit.
3. **A plan built on the results**, discussed with you before anything is
   scheduled.

Nothing is placed and nothing is prescribed before step three.
