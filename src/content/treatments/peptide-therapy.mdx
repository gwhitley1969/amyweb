## What it is

Peptides are short chains of amino acids — the same building blocks that make
up proteins. Some, like glutathione and NAD+, the body already makes on its
own; others are made synthetically. In a clinical setting they're used in a
range of supervised treatments, usually given as a small injection.

Amy offers a defined set, shown above. Where the internet reaches for sweeping
claims, her approach is deliberately plain: what each peptide is, how it's
given, and whether it belongs in your plan at all — decided together, in
consultation.

The peptides she offers span a few kinds. **Copper peptides** — GHK-Cu, and
GLOW, a blend built around it. **Growth-hormone-releasing peptides** —
Ipamorelin, Sermorelin, and Tesamorelin. **A synthetic blend** — BPC-157 with
TB-500. And **compounds the body makes on its own** — Glutathione, NAD+, and
MOTS-c.
