## What they are

Two clinician-performed approaches to skin texture and tone — one device,
one solution. **PiXel8-RF** pairs fine microneedles with radiofrequency
energy in a single treatment. **Medical-grade chemical peels** are
clinician-applied solutions that exfoliate the outer layer of the skin.
Different tools, same idea: rather than covering anything up, both are
designed to work with the skin's own renewal.

## How PiXel8-RF works

PiXel8-RF, from Rohrer Aesthetics, is an FDA-cleared device that combines
two things in one pass. The microneedles create micro-channels in the
skin's surface; the 4 MHz radiofrequency energy travels through those
needles and warms the deeper dermal layer — the layer where collagen and
elastin are made. The combination is designed to prompt the skin's own
production of both.

It's used where the concern is the appearance of skin laxity and
crepiness, uneven tone and texture, acne scarring, or stretch marks — and
it isn't limited to the face. Neck, jawline, and body areas are all within
its range, and the device is designed for all skin types and tones.
Whether it's the right tool for *your* skin is a consultation
conversation.

## Chemical peels

A chemical peel is the older idea, refined: a clinician-applied solution
that exfoliates the outer layer of the skin, applied and monitored by Amy
in the studio. Peels start at $180, and they differ — in depth, in
ingredients, in what they ask of your calendar afterward. Which peel — and
whether a peel or PiXel8-RF fits your skin better — is a consultation
conversation, and the full peel menu is something Amy walks through in
person.

## A longer view

If the idea of building your skin's own collagen appeals — gradual change,
